Get away from the city

A hidden gem in the beautiful village of Moretonhampstead

Relax and unwind with a comfy bed and a chef-cooked breakfast


A BnB situated in the heart of The Moor.



Crossroads BnB is close to the beautiful Dartmoor Moors, with fantastic views for miles. Moretonhampstead is a small village on the edge of the moor which is around 10 mins drive away and a 1/2 hour walk. The village has a couple of pubs and eateries on offer, The White Hart is a lovely hotel with a bar, restaurant open to the public, meals are very reasonable and the bar has lovely bar meals too. Afternoon teas can be taken here in the lounge or Garden. We have also The Horse, lovely surroundings inside, restaurant to the rear with a small enclosed garden, it offers fabulous food, pizzas and good selection of local beers. During the summer months we have the odd music and quiz night. The Old Union Inn is a pub with character, lovely food, fantastic selection of beer and a lovely outside space on a sunny day, very reasonably priced would recommend booking at weekends, the same with The Horse they get very busy. Roberto's Pizza restaurant, fabulous atmosphere and food, small but worth it! Roberto's does not have an alcohol licence so please take your own along, they will provide the glasses and soft drinks are available. Booking is always recommended as a lot of the locals use this one. We also have our Lovely Tea Rooms, only recently renovated and beautifully done too. well recommend this one for afternoon teas and light lunches. If your wanting a sit in the sun and a lovely cup of coffee and a good lunch then Central Stores Cafe is ideal, situated next to the Information centre and our lovely Brainwaves charity shop where you can always find a fantastic bargain.

Nearby


Moretonhampstead is not far from some wonderful places to visit; Bovey Tracy, Bovey Castle, Newton Abbot town and racecourse, Torquay, Paignton, Dawlish, Teignmouth, Widecombe-in-the-Moor, Exeter Cathedral and city, Sidmouth, Exmouth (the start of the Jurassic coastline) and Plymouth (the start of Cornwall). Crossroads BnB is only 1.5 hours away from the fantastic EDEN project and the lost gardens of Heligan. Brixham and Dartmouth are also only a 50 minute drive. Dartmouth has its round robin, boats, steam cruise and steam trains, Agatha Christie's house Greenway and gardens. I could go on and on and on you would never get bored! Brixham is a wonderful seaside harbour town, with a fantastic fish and chip shop on the Harbour front worth a visit.

Moretonhampstead has its own seasonal open air swimming pool. We have a lovely Green Hill Art/Craft centre which has some fantastic workshops and exhibitions on throughout the year. Ella's beauty centre is also here; she offers massages, manicures and facials (booking required). We also have a fantastic motorcar and bike museum in the village, which is well worth a visit. Moretonhampstead holds a Carnival festival every year in August, which features an entire week of entertainment and music. We also have a local sports centre, shops, butchers and hairdressers.

Then you have the beautiful Dartmoor National Park, Haytor rock, Hound Tor, horse riding, rock climbing, mountain biking, fantastic views and walks you have over 368 miles to cover why not lunch at the Rock inn or the Cafe on the Green at Widecombe in the Moor or drink in the view of the fantastic Ten Commandments stone, or you could head down to Dartmeet and have your picture taken as a pixie in pixie land, Drive to Badgers Halt and Spitchwick cool of in the river and grab yourself a lovely afternoon tea at the cafe. Or join us in September for the famous Widecombe fair and old Uncle Tom Cobly.

Crossroads BnB has been newly renovated, the rooms are de luxe doubles featuring en suites and a small dressing/sitting area. Tea, coffee and toiletries are complimentary. If you have forgotten anything please let us know we may be able to help. Hairdryers can be found in the room. Breakfast is set up in the large lounge, if you wish to have it in private then continental can be served in your room with prebooking the day before. The building is no-smoking and no-vaping zone, there is a small garden should you need to and there is plenty of outside space in the village. Parking in either Station Road or Court Street are charged between the start of Easter and the end of October, up until then and after it is free parking. Many of the street parking areas are 1 hour some of 1/2 hour, please be aware that we do have traffic wardens. Street parking is 8am to 5pm after that its free, if you park in one of these at 5pm you need to move it before 9am unless its a Sunday where parking all day is free.

During the summer months we have our Secret Garden days, local people open up their gardens once a year for local charities. They are all fantastic and beautiful to sit and have a slice of cake and a cup of tea in such tranquil surroundings.
